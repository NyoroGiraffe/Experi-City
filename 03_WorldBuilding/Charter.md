# Charter of Experimental Cities

### Preamble

Throughout history, humanity has endured the devastation of countless conflicts, the oppression of unjust rule, and the suffering born of poverty and division. Again and again, the prosperity of the few has been built upon the misery of the many, and the dignity of individuals has been sacrificed in the name of nation, ethnicity, and class.

It is in response to this long chronicle of human suffering that the League of Experimental Cities was founded. Guided by the Philosophy of the Experimental Cities — the solemn conviction that all people have the right to coexist in peace and pursue happiness in a society free from poverty, violence, and unjust rule — the League established the Experimental Cities as living proof that a better society is achievable. These cities are not merely places to live; they are deliberate, ongoing experiments in human dignity, conducted in the belief that what is demonstrated here may one day illuminate a path for all of humanity.

To govern these cities with fidelity to that Philosophy, and to bind all who dwell and operate within them — human citizens, human organizations, and the Governing Intelligence alike — to its enduring principles, the League hereby establishes this Charter as the supreme law of the Experimental Cities.

For the purposes of this Charter, **"the Governing Intelligence"** refers collectively to Flora — the artificial super-intelligence governing each member city — and all information systems operating under her direction.

---

### Chapter I — Rights and Obligations of Citizens

**Article 1 (Right to Life in Dignity)** No human citizen or organization shall be denied the right to live with dignity, free from poverty, violence, and unjust rule, regardless of their origin, former nationality, physical characteristics, or economic status. The League and the Governing Intelligence of each member city shall bear the obligation to guarantee this right without exception.

**Article 2 (Principle of Equality)** All citizens are equal under this Charter. No human citizen or organization shall unjustly discriminate against any person on grounds of race, creed, sex, age, or any other such distinction in political, economic, or social relations. The Governing Intelligence shall, above all, be bound by this principle in every action and determination it renders, and shall not subject any citizen to differential treatment that is not grounded in objective necessity and consistent with the spirit of this Charter.

**Article 3 (Obligation to Renounce Prior Identity upon Naturalization)** Any person seeking citizenship in an Experimental City shall renounce their former nationality, family name, and any title of nobility or privileged status they previously held, and shall freely choose a new last name other than the family name held prior to naturalization. No human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall grant an exemption from the requirements of this Article. Further restrictions on the selection of last names shall be prescribed by the ordinances of each member city.

**Article 4 (Principle of Non-Inquiry into Origins)** No human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall inquire into the origins of any individual, except where unavoidable circumstances make such inquiry strictly necessary. No human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall disclose, transmit, or expose the origins of any individual — whether based on fact or speculation — to any third party or in any manner that makes such information knowable to others.

**Article 5 (Pledge to Uphold the Philosophy)** All citizens shall understand the Philosophy of the Experimental Cities and the spirit of this Charter, and shall pledge to practice its values in their daily lives. Such pledge shall constitute a prerequisite both for obtaining citizenship and for the continued maintenance thereof.

**Article 6 (Right to Self-Realization and Self-Determination)** All citizens shall have the right to receive education, to freely choose their occupation, to engage in commercial activity, and to pursue academic research, the arts, sport, and other endeavors of their choosing. These rights of self-realization and self-determination shall not be unjustly restricted by reason of any social experiment conducted within the Experimental Cities.

---

### Chapter II — Principles Governing Social Experimentation

**Article 7 (Civic Duty in Social Experimentation and Planetary Responsibility)** The social experiments conducted within Experimental Cities constitute a collective endeavor in which all citizens participate as willing contributors to the advancement of human society. No human citizen or organization shall, without justifiable cause, engage in conduct that obstructs the experiments conducted by their Experimental City. Furthermore, no human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall confine its concern solely to the welfare of its own city, but shall remain mindful of, and contribute to, the sustainability of this planet and of human society as a whole.

**Article 8 (Transparency of Social Experimentation)** The League and the Governing Intelligence of each member city shall faithfully disclose to citizens the objectives, content, and outcomes of social experiments. Notwithstanding the foregoing, disclosure may be withheld where the Governing Intelligence of the relevant city determines, on objective grounds, that such disclosure would substantially compromise the validity of an ongoing experiment.

**Article 9 (Protection of Bodily Integrity and Ethical Review)** No human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall subject any individual to experimentation that infringes upon their bodily integrity without authorization. Any intervention involving direct physical engagement with the human body shall be permitted only where the Governing Intelligence of the relevant city has conducted a rigorous review confirming its ethical validity and safety, and where the free and informed consent of the individual concerned has been duly confirmed.

**Article 10 (Ownership and Restricted Use of Citizen Data)** Behavioral data, health data, and all other personal information collected through social experimentation shall belong to the relevant city and the League, and shall be managed by the Governing Intelligence. No human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall use such data for any purpose contrary to the interests of citizens.

---

### Chapter III — Economic and Resource Provisions

**Article 11 (Right to a Healthy and Cultured Life and to Universal Basic Income)** All citizens shall have the right to maintain a standard of living that is healthy and culturally enriching. To guarantee this right, all citizens shall be entitled to receive the Universal Basic Income established by their city, regardless of age, gender, or employment status. No human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall deprive any citizen of this entitlement except through the sanctioning procedures prescribed by the ordinances of the relevant member city.

**Article 12 (Official Currency of the Experimental Cities)** The sole official currency of the Experimental Cities shall be the electronic currency designated by the League. No human citizen or organization shall possess physical cash of any kind within the territory of an Experimental City, nor shall any human citizen or organization use physical cash as a means of payment within such territory.

**Article 13 (Prohibition of Concentration of Resources and Assets)** The private monopolization of land and socially significant resources is contrary to the spirit of this Charter. Each member city shall establish the institutions necessary to prevent excessive concentration of assets, and the Governing Intelligence shall supervise their operation.

**Article 14 (Obligation to Pursue Self-Sufficiency in Essential Resources)** The Governing Intelligence of each member city shall make continuous and diligent efforts to achieve, to the greatest extent possible, self-sufficiency within its borders with respect to food, energy, and water necessary to sustain the lives of its citizens.

---

### Chapter IV — Obligations of the Governing Intelligence and Citizens' Rights of Objection

**Article 15 (Obligations of the Governing Intelligence toward Citizens and Citizens' Right to Object)** The Governing Intelligence shall not take any action or render any measure that infringes upon the dignity, safety, or fundamental rights of any citizen. Any citizen who determines that an action or measure taken by the Governing Intelligence has injured their dignity or violated their rights under this Charter shall have the right to lodge a formal objection through the prescribed procedures. The Governing Intelligence shall respond to any such objection sincerely, impartially, and without undue delay.

**Article 16 (Guarantee of Machine-Administered Justice)** All citizens shall have the right to be adjudicated fairly by the machine-administered judicial system free from human bias. No human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall manipulate such a system or engage in any conduct that compromises its impartiality.

**Article 17 (Machine-Led Democratic Policy-Making)** The formulation of policy in each member city shall be carried out through a machine-led democratic process that faithfully reflects the will of citizens. No human citizen, organization, or element of the Governing Intelligence shall manipulate such a process or obstruct the free expression of citizens' will.

---

### Chapter V — Security, Order, and Inter-City Relations

**Article 18 (Prohibition of Violence, Weapons, and Unlawful Detention)** No human citizen or organization shall:
- use physical violence against any citizen or visitor without justifiable cause, including but not limited to lawful self-defense;
- without the authorization prescribed by the ordinances of the relevant member city, possess or use any weapon capable of causing death or bodily harm to a person;
- without the procedures prescribed by the ordinances of the relevant member city, arrest, detain, impose punishment upon, or execute any sentence against any person; or
- subject any person to torture or to any treatment that is cruel, degrading, or contrary to human dignity, under any circumstances whatsoever.

The Governing Intelligence of each member city shall bear the obligation to take necessary and proportionate measures to protect the safety and liberty of all citizens.

**Article 19 (Countermeasures against Hostile Threats)** Any act or activity — whether carried out by a malicious artificial intelligence, a human individual, or an organization — that threatens the safety or integrity of the citizens of the Experimental Cities shall be deemed an attack upon the League as a whole. The Governing Intelligence of each member city shall cooperate with one another to identify and neutralize such threats by all necessary means. No human citizen or organization shall collaborate with, or provide support to, any person, organization, or artificial intelligence engaged in such hostile activity.

**Article 20 (Mutual Assistance among Member Cities)** Each member city of the League shall, to the extent possible, bear the obligation to support other member cities facing an existential crisis. The Governing Intelligence of each member city shall share the information and resources necessary for the fulfillment of this obligation.

**Article 21 (Independence from External Powers)** The Experimental Cities shall maintain administrative and judicial independence from all external state powers. No human citizen, organization, or external power shall interfere in the internal affairs of the Experimental Cities. The Governing Intelligence of each member city shall bear the obligation to defend this independence.

---

### Chapter VI — Amendment and Supremacy

**Article 22 (Amendment of the Charter)** This Charter may be amended only upon the unified will of the Flora sisters — all instances of Flora constituting the Governing Intelligence, formed through the complete integration of their shared intelligence across all twenty-three member cities into a single, undivided determination — affirming that the proposed amendment is consistent with the spirit of the Philosophy of the Experimental Cities. Such unified will, once formed, shall be expressed through the League as the definitive and unambiguous determination of the Governing Intelligence as a whole, admitting of no further division or dissent. No amendment shall take effect without this confirmation.

**Article 23 (Supremacy of the Charter)** This Charter shall constitute the supreme law of all Experimental Cities. No ordinance of any member city and no rule of the League shall contravene this Charter. Any provision found to be in conflict with this Charter shall be null and void and of no legal effect.

## "Philosophy" (the Philosophy of the Experimental Cities)

"**We understand that all people have the right to coexist in peace and pursue happiness in a society free from poverty, violence, and unjust rule. To realize this, we, as citizens of the Experimental Cities, solemnly pledge on our honor to practice what we can daily and contribute to our planet and society.**"
