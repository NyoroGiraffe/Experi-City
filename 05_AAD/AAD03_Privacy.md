# Against AI Dystopia 03: Privacy

### Concern:

Surveillance will destroy privacy.

### Response (Short):

Privacy is reduced, but reframed as protection from human misuse rather than from observation itself.

### Response (Why it works in Experi-City):

In Experi-City, citizens are continuously observed through connected systems that track health, behavior, and money use. From a normal point of view, this looks like a clear loss of privacy. However, the system makes a strong difference between being observed and being misused. Data is not treated as something to buy and sell or to gain personal advantage, but as a shared resource managed by AI systems that have no personal desires. People accept less privacy not because they do not care, but because they trust that the system does not act with bias, greed, or hidden goals. The principle of Non-Inquiry into Origins also stops people from using personal history against others, and system control reduces unfair human judgment. In this model, privacy is no longer mainly about hiding information, but about being protected from harmful use by other people.
